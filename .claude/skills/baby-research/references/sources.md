# Curated Source List

Sources are organized by category and ranked by editorial independence and methodological rigor. Use site-specific WebSearch queries to pull from these sources first.

## Gear (car seats, strollers, cribs, carriers, etc.)

### Tier 1 — Independent testing, no affiliate revenue

| Source | Why trusted | Best for | Search pattern |
|--------|------------|----------|----------------|
| **Consumer Reports** | Nonprofit, actual lab/crash testing, zero affiliate revenue, subscription-funded | Car seats (crash testing), cribs (structural integrity), strollers (durability) | `site:consumerreports.org "infant car seat"` |
| **NHTSA** | Federal agency, ease-of-use ratings, recall database | Car seat ease-of-use ratings, vehicle compatibility, recalls | `site:nhtsa.gov car seat` |
| **CPSC** | Federal agency, safety standards enforcement, recall database | Recall lookups (check ANY product before buying, especially used), safety standards | `site:cpsc.gov recall [product]` or `site:saferproducts.gov [product]` |

### Tier 2 — Strong methodology, affiliate revenue disclosed

| Source | Why trusted | Bias note | Best for | Search pattern |
|--------|------------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| **Wirecutter (NYT)** | Hands-on testing, expert interviews, NYT editorial standards | Affiliate commission model — revenue depends on purchases through their links. Picks frequently align with CR, suggesting affiliate model doesn't fully corrupt results, but the structural conflict is real | General gear, usability focus, "best overall" picks | `site:nytimes.com/wirecutter "best [product]"` |
| **BabyGearLab** | Structured scoring rubrics, hands-on testing with real families, detailed breakdowns | Affiliate links (Amazon, etc.). More transparent than most affiliate sites due to published rubrics | Strollers, carriers, car seat usability | `site:babygearlab.com [product] review` |
| **Car Seat Lady** | Run by Alisa Baer, MD + certified CPST. Specialized expertise in installation, fit, and safety nuance | Minor affiliate links. Clinical background + CPST cert is a meaningful differentiator | Car seat selection, installation guidance, harness fit, vehicle compatibility | `site:thecarseatlady.com [topic]` |

### Tier 3 — Certification/standards (not review sources)

| Source | Use for | Note |
|--------|---------|------|
| **JPMA** | Verifying product meets ASTM standards (JPMA seal) | Industry-funded — use as safety floor check, not recommendation source |
| **Safe Kids Worldwide** | Finding local car seat inspection events | Nonprofit. J&J-founded but research arm is credible |

### General search patterns for gear
```
"best infant car seat 2025" OR "best infant car seat 2026"
"[product] review" site:consumerreports.org OR site:nytimes.com/wirecutter
"[product] recall" site:cpsc.gov
"[product] ease of use rating" site:nhtsa.gov
```

## Health & Safety (sleep, vaccines, newborn care, development)

### Tier 1 — Clinical guidelines and systematic reviews

| Source | Why trusted | Best for | Caveats | Search pattern |
|--------|------------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **AAP / HealthyChildren.org** | Gold standard US pediatric guidelines, evidence-graded | Safe sleep (2022 update), vaccines, car seat positioning, developmental screening | Room-sharing >6mo has weaker evidence than 0-6mo. Screen time guidance is correlational. Breastfeeding framing sometimes underweights maternal mental health | `site:publications.aap.org [topic]` or `site:healthychildren.org [topic]` |
| **Cochrane Reviews** | Pre-registered systematic reviews with GRADE ratings, strong editorial independence | "What do the RCTs actually show?" on clinical questions — epidurals, skin-to-skin, pacifiers, breastfeeding interventions, vitamin D | Only as good as underlying trials (often small in peds). Reviews can be outdated — check date | `site:cochranelibrary.com [topic]` |
| **CDC** | US government public health agency | Childhood immunization schedule (co-authored with AAP), developmental milestones (2022 update), lead exposure, newborn screening | Not a clinical care source. 2022 milestone revision is contested by some clinicians | `site:cdc.gov [topic] infant` |
| **ACOG** | Evidence-graded practice bulletins, essential for pregnancy | Prenatal care, labor/delivery, postpartum, high-risk pregnancy | More intervention-oriented than midwifery orgs. Scope ends at delivery | `site:acog.org [topic]` |

### Tier 2 — Specialized evidence sources

| Source | Why trusted | Best for | Search pattern |
|--------|------------|----------|----------------|
| **Zero to Three** | Nonprofit, developmental psychology research | Early development 0-3, social-emotional milestones, screen time nuance, attachment | `site:zerotothree.org [topic]` |
| **LactMed (NIH)** | Government database, no industry funding, regularly updated | Drug/supplement safety during breastfeeding — definitive source | `site:ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK501922 [drug name]` |
| **UpToDate** | Physician topic editors, evidence-graded, conflicts disclosed | Pregnancy complications, specific medical conditions | Paywalled. Written for clinicians. `site:uptodate.com [topic]` |

### General search patterns for health
```
"[topic] AAP guidelines" OR "[topic] AAP policy statement"
"[topic] cochrane review"
"[topic] infant" site:cdc.gov
"[topic] SIDS" OR "[topic] safe sleep" site:publications.aap.org
```

## Feeding (breastfeeding, formula, pumping, solids)

### Tier 1 — Clinical guidelines

| Source | Why trusted | Best for | Caveats | Search pattern |
|--------|------------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **AAP** | Official US feeding guidelines | Exclusive breastfeeding duration, early allergen introduction (reversed old guidance), formula safety, complementary foods | Can lag emerging research 2-5 years. 2022 update improved but still sometimes underweights breastfeeding difficulty | `site:publications.aap.org breastfeeding` or `site:healthychildren.org feeding` |
| **ABM (Academy of Breastfeeding Medicine)** | International physician org, free evidence-graded clinical protocols | Mastitis, milk supply, latch, medications, jaundice, supplementation decisions, pumping at work | More clinically detailed than AAP or WHO on lactation specifics | `site:bfmed.org protocol` |
| **LactMed (NIH)** | Government database, definitive | Any medication question during breastfeeding | Narrow scope (medications only) | `site:ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK501922 [drug]` |

### Tier 2 — Evidence-based practitioner sources

| Source | Why trusted | Best for | Caveats | Search pattern |
|--------|------------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **KellyMom** | Run by IBCLC, cites primary literature and AAP/WHO | Breastfeeding mechanics (latch, supply, pumping, storage), returning to work | Pro-breastfeeding orientation — great for BF support, not balanced on formula decisions. Some pages not updated since 2018 — check dates | `site:kellymom.com [topic]` |
| **InfantRisk Center** | Texas Tech research center, run by Dr. Thomas Hale (author of clinical reference) | Medication safety in pregnancy and breastfeeding, has phone hotline | Narrow scope | `site:infantrisk.com [topic]` |
| **FARE** | Food allergy research nonprofit | Allergen introduction protocols, especially peanut (based on LEAP trial RCT) | Narrow scope | `site:foodallergy.org [allergen] introduction` |

### Tier 3 — Practical guides

| Source | Why trusted | Best for | Caveats |
|--------|------------|----------|---------|
| **Solid Starts** | Founded by pediatric feeding specialist (OT), food-by-food database | Baby-led weaning food prep, age-appropriate textures | Has affiliate links. Content quality is high but it's a commercial site |
| **WHO** | Global health authority, GRADE methodology | Cross-referencing AAP on breastfeeding duration | Calibrated for global populations including resource-limited settings — "no formula unless necessary" framing doesn't apply the same way in the US with safe water |

## Childcare & Parenting Decisions

### Tier 1 — Evidence synthesis

| Source | Why trusted | Best for | Caveats | Search pattern |
|--------|------------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Emily Oster / ParentData** | Economist (Brown), statistical literacy, synthesizes peer-reviewed lit | Contested decisions (sleep training, breastfeeding evidence, screen time, childcare), quantifying risk | Not a physician — occasionally misses clinical nuance. Alcohol-in-pregnancy position drew criticism. Newsletter quality varies | `site:parentdata.org [topic]` |
| **Cochrane Reviews** | Systematic reviews | Clinical questions with RCT evidence | Same as above | `site:cochranelibrary.com [topic]` |
| **AAP** | Official recommendations | Standard of care positions | Same as above | (same patterns) |

### Tier 2 — Childcare-specific

| Source | Why trusted | Best for |
|--------|------------|----------|
| **NICHD Study of Early Child Care** | Definitive US longitudinal study (1,364 families) | Key finding: quality of caregiving > setting type (daycare vs nanny vs family). Quality = ratio, turnover, language engagement, training |
| **NAEYC** | Sets voluntary accreditation standards for childcare centers | Quality filter when evaluating daycares — NAEYC accreditation is a meaningful signal |
| **Child Care Aware of America** | Tracks childcare costs, licensing, availability by state | Market research for your specific area |
| **Craig Canapari, MD** | Yale pediatric sleep specialist, evidence-cited | Sleep training evidence and specific sleep situations |

### Tier 3 — Books (evidence-based)

| Book | Author | Best for |
|------|--------|----------|
| *Cribsheet* | Emily Oster | Evidence on contested parenting decisions — read before baby arrives |
| *Expecting Better* | Emily Oster | Pregnancy decisions |
| *The Gardener and the Carpenter* | Alison Gopnik | Developmental psychology for parents |
| *It's Never Too Late to Sleep Train* | Craig Canapari, MD | Sleep training evidence and methods |

## Sources NOT to Use

These are explicitly excluded from the curated list:

| Source type | Why excluded |
|-------------|-------------|
| Reddit (r/beyondthebump, r/NewParents, etc.) | Anecdotal, no editorial standards, survivorship bias |
| Parenting forums (BabyCenter, What to Expect forums) | Same as Reddit |
| Social media (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook groups) | Unvetted, algorithm-driven, influencer economics |
| Affiliate-heavy review sites (not Wirecutter/BGL) | Revenue model corrupts recommendations |
| Mommy blogs | No transparent methodology |
| Fear-based health sites | Designed to drive engagement, not inform |
| Manufacturer marketing | Use for specs only, never for safety or efficacy claims |
