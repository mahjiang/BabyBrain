# Stroller

> **Status:** Researching | **Buy by:** Week 30-32

## Our Requirements

- **Car seat compatible** — Must work with chosen infant seat (see [car seat](../../02-gear/car-seat.md))
- **Transit friendly** — Easy to fold one-handed, compact fold, lightweight enough to carry up/down stairs
- **Car friendly** — Fits in our trunk
- **Apartment friendly** — Compact storage footprint
- **Handles city terrain** — Sidewalks, curbs, possible rough pavement

## Types to Consider

| Type | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|------|----------|------|------|
| **Travel system** | Simplicity | Car seat + stroller matched; one purchase | Often bulky, heavy |
| **Full-size + adapter** | Longevity | Best ride, features, lasts years | Heavy, expensive |
| **Lightweight / umbrella** | Transit | Light, compact fold | Less sturdy, fewer features |
| **Compact / city stroller** | Apartment + transit | Small fold, light, still full-featured | Pricier for quality ones |

### Car Seat Compatibility Key Point

*Evidence:* Car seat compatibility is the primary situational constraint — the stroller decision depends on the car seat decision. Key compatibility patterns:

- **Nuna PIPA series** (the lightest infant car seat category) pairs natively with Nuna TRVL — no adapter needed, seamless click-in. Also compatible via Maxi-Cosi adapter with most other strollers.
- **Chicco KeyFit 30/35** is compatible with Baby Jogger via adapter; Baby Jogger has the broadest adapter ecosystem of any stroller brand.
- **Virtually all infant car seats** work with a Maxi-Cosi-style adapter, meaning most quality strollers can work with most car seats.

> **Action needed:** Finalize car seat choice before committing to a stroller — see [car-seat.md](../../02-gear/car-seat.md). If going with Nuna PIPA, the Nuna TRVL travel system is a compelling pairing.

(Source: [Babylist Car Seat Stroller Compatibility Guide](https://www.babylist.com/hello-baby/car-seat-stroller-compatibility))

### Transit Weight: The Real Constraint

*Opinion (Eric's situation analysis):* At 20+ lb, a stroller on subway stairs means: lift stroller + lift/secure baby. At 13 lb, that's meaningful. In practice, many city parents find themselves carrying the stroller more than they expected.

Weight categories to think about:
- **Under 15 lb** — can realistically manage one-handed on stairs while other hand steadies railing
- **15–20 lb** — doable but awkward; depends on upper body strength and frequency
- **20+ lb** — most parents avoid stairs with these; elevator or bus only

The Nuna TRVL (13.6 lb) and Joolz Aer+ (13.2 lb) are in the "manageable" category. The City Mini GT2 (21.4 lb) is at the threshold where transit becomes a conscious effort. The Cruz V2 (25.5 lb) and Mockingbird (27 lb) effectively commit you to elevator use.

---

### Terrain Performance

*Evidence (BabyGearLab testing):* The Joolz Aer+ "doesn't perform so well on rougher ground." The City Mini GT2 with forever-air rubber tires and all-wheel suspension handles uneven sidewalks well. The Nuna TRVL performs well on most urban surfaces but is not an all-terrain product.

For NYC-style urban use (variable sidewalk quality, curbs, subway elevators): the City Mini GT2 actually handles real-world city terrain better than pure travel strollers, which are optimized for airports more than broken sidewalks.
