## Our Requirements

- **Car seat compatible** — Must work with chosen infant seat (see [car seat](car-seat.md))
- **Transit friendly** — Easy to fold one-handed, compact fold, lightweight enough to carry up/down stairs
- **Car friendly** — Fits in our trunk
- **Apartment friendly** — Compact storage footprint
- **Handles city terrain** — Sidewalks, curbs, possible rough pavement

## Types to Consider

| Type | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|------|----------|------|------|
| **Travel system** | Simplicity | Car seat + stroller matched; one purchase | Often bulky, heavy |
| **Full-size + adapter** | Longevity | Best ride, features, lasts years | Heavy, expensive |
| **Lightweight / umbrella** | Transit | Light, compact fold | Less sturdy, fewer features |
| **Compact / city stroller** | Apartment + transit | Small fold, light, still full-featured | Pricier for quality ones |

## Research & Evidence

*Added 2026-02-28:*

### Weight and Transit Use

- For stair-carrying in transit, most experts and parent communities recommend targeting **under 15 lbs** for a primary stroller. Under 14 lbs is ideal if stairs are frequent.
- One-hand fold is nearly essential for transit — you need one hand free for the baby or a handrail.
- *Anecdotal:* Parent communities (r/beyondthebump, r/NewParents) consistently rate the YOYO2 and Bugaboo Butterfly as top picks for subway/transit cities. The MINU v2 comes up frequently for urban use with a car.

### Car Seat Compatibility

- **Travel systems** (same-brand car seat + stroller) offer the most seamless infant-seat transfer: car seat clicks in without an adapter.
- **Adapter compatibility** is generally excellent for the major infant seat brands (Nuna PIPA, Chicco KeyFit, UPPAbaby MESA, Cybex Cloud) — but always verify the specific adapter before buying.
- UPPAbaby MESA + MINU v2 is a popular same-ecosystem pairing (both UPPAbaby; no adapter needed). Worth considering if car seat decision lands on MESA.
- Nuna PIPA + TRVL is similarly seamless.

### Fold Size and Apartment Storage

- The **Bugaboo Butterfly** and **YOYO2** have the smallest folded footprints of full-featured strollers. Both can fit in a closet vertically.
- The **GB Pockit+** is the most extreme option for storage — folds to roughly the size of a large purse — but compromises on canopy size and ride quality.
- Most "compact city" strollers (MINU, TRVL, Ergobaby Metro+) stand upright when folded, which is good for apartment hallway/closet storage.

### Terrain and Ride Quality

- Urban sidewalks with curbs and cracks: look for foam-filled or air-filled tires and a front-wheel suspension system.
- The **Baby Jogger City Mini GT2** handles rougher terrain best of the options listed (all-terrain tires) but is heavier.
- The **UPPAbaby MINU v2** and **TRVL** handle typical urban sidewalks well but are not true all-terrain strollers.
- The **YOYO2** and **Butterfly** are smooth on pavement but noticeably less comfortable on very rough surfaces.

### Price and Registry Strategy

- High-end compact strollers ($500–$700) are strong registry candidates — family members often want a "big ticket" item to buy together.
- The **GB Pockit+** is the value pick if budget is the top constraint.
- *Opinion:* The Babyzen YOYO2 at ~$600 is expensive but has a strong resale market (~$350–$450 used) if you want to sell after the toddler phase.
- Consider buying the frame/stroller new but getting the extra accessories (cup holder, rain cover) used or skipping them.

### Safety Notes

- No major safety distinctions between the listed options — all are JPMA certified and meet ASTM standards.
- Strollers are **not** a safety-critical item in the same way as car seats; used strollers are generally fine if structurally sound, hardware intact, and no recalls. (Source: [CPSC stroller recall database](https://www.cpsc.gov/Recalls))
- Always verify no active recalls before buying used.
