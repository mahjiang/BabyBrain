# Registry Strategy

> **Status:** Researching | **Priority:** HIGH — Set up before week 24

## Philosophy

A registry isn't just a gift list — it's a research tool, price tracker, and completion-discount strategy. The goal: get the best stuff at the best prices with minimal waste.

## Where to Register

| Store | Completion Discount | Return Window | Why Use It |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|------------|
| **Amazon** | 15% (Prime) / 10% | 90 days | Widest selection, universal add-on, easy for out-of-town gifters |
| **Target** | 15% | 1 year (registry items) | Good for basics, easy returns, free welcome kit |
| **Babylist** | 15% | Varies by retailer | Aggregates from any store, flexible cash funds, most user-friendly |

### Recommendation

> **Register at 2 places max** to keep it manageable. Consider: **Babylist** (as the shareable primary, since it pulls from anywhere) + **Amazon** (for the completion discount on bigger items).

## Strategy: What to Register For vs Self-Buy vs Skip

### Register For (Let People Gift These)
- Clothes (people LOVE buying baby clothes)
- Swaddles, burp cloths, bibs
- Books and toys
- Diaper supply / diaper fund
- Stroller (if someone wants to go big)
- Carrier/wrap
- Nursing pillow, bottles
- Fun/cute items that aren't safety-critical

### Self-Buy (Research and Buy Yourself)
- **Car seat** — Safety-critical; you need to research fit for your car
- **Crib/bassinet** — Safety-critical; needs to fit your space
- **Breast pump** — Usually covered by insurance
- **Monitor** — Personal preference varies widely
- Mattress (must meet safety standards; don't buy used)

### Skip / Wait
- Shoes (they don't walk for ~12 months)
- Baby food maker (a regular blender works)
- Wipe warmer (nice-to-have at best)
- Newborn-sized clothing (they outgrow it in days-weeks)
- Baby bath tub (a sink or regular tub works for many families)

## Completion Discount Strategy

Most registries offer a one-time 10-15% discount on remaining items after a certain date (usually 60 days before due date or after the shower).

**Strategy:**
1. Add everything you *might* want to the registry — even stuff you plan to buy yourself
2. Let the shower happen
3. After the shower, assess what you still need
4. Use the completion discount to buy remaining big-ticket items at 10-15% off
5. Time it right — discounts often expire, and some are one-time-use

- [ ] Note completion discount dates for each registry
- [ ] Plan completion discount purchase list post-shower

## Baby Shower Logistics

- Typical timing: weeks 28-34
- Share registry link early with shower host
- Include a mix of price points ($10-$200+)
- Consider adding a "diaper fund" or "book instead of card" option
