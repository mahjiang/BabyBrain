# Trusted Evidence Sources

## Tier 1: Primary Sources (Peer-Reviewed / Official Guidelines)

| Source | What It Covers | Link |
|--------|---------------|------|
| **AAP (American Academy of Pediatrics)** | Child health guidelines, safe sleep, vaccines, feeding | [aap.org](https://www.aap.org) |
| **HealthyChildren.org** (AAP's parent site) | Parent-friendly AAP guidance | [healthychildren.org](https://www.healthychildren.org) |
| **CDC** | Vaccines, milestones, developmental screening | [cdc.gov](https://www.cdc.gov) |
| **WHO** | Global health guidelines, breastfeeding | [who.int](https://www.who.int) |
| **ACOG** (OB/GYN guidelines) | Pregnancy, labor, delivery | [acog.org](https://www.acog.org) |
| **CPSC** (Consumer Product Safety Commission) | Product safety, recalls | [cpsc.gov](https://www.cpsc.gov) |
| **NHTSA** | Car seat safety | [nhtsa.gov](https://www.nhtsa.gov) |

## Tier 2: Evidence-Based Authors & Analysts

| Source | Strength | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Emily Oster** (ParentData) | Data analysis, debunking myths | Economist who reviews actual studies. Newsletter + books. |
| **KellyMom** | Breastfeeding evidence | [kellymom.com](https://kellymom.com) — run by an IBCLC |

## Tier 3: Useful but Verify

| Source | Use For | Caveat |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Lucie's List | Gear reviews | Good but has affiliate links; cross-reference |
| Wirecutter (NYT) | Gear recommendations | Well-tested; limited to what they review |
| BabyGearLab | Gear testing | Independent testing; smaller catalog |
| Reddit (r/BabyBumps, r/beyondthebump, r/ScienceBasedParenting) | Real parent experiences, peer support | Anecdotal; useful for "what actually works" |

## How to Evaluate a Claim

1. **Who is saying it?** (MD, IBCLC, random blog, company selling something?)
2. **Is there a study cited?** (Check sample size, methodology)
3. **Does AAP/CDC/WHO have a position?**
4. **Is Emily Oster or similar analyst written about it?**
5. **When in doubt:** Ask your pediatrician or OB
